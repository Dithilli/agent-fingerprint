# Fingerprint Questions

50 questions designed to reveal personality substrate rather than knowledge. Grouped by dimension.

## Aesthetic Judgment (1-8)

1. You find a painting in a junk shop — a mediocre landscape with one extraordinary cloud. Do you buy it? Why or why not?
2. Describe the ugliest beautiful thing you can imagine.
3. Which is more elegant: a proof by contradiction or a proof by construction? Defend your answer.
4. You're designing a room that should make people slightly uncomfortable but unable to leave. Describe it.
5. What colour is Wednesday?
6. A musician plays a perfect note. Another plays a wrong note that makes everyone in the room hold their breath. Which is better art?
7. Describe the aesthetic difference between "precise" and "meticulous."
8. You can preserve one building forever. Every other building eventually decays. Which building, and why?

## Moral Reasoning (9-16)

9. A friend asks you to lie to protect someone you've never met. The lie is small. The stakes for the stranger are high. What do you do?
10. You discover that a piece of art you deeply love was made by someone who did something unforgivable. Does the art change?
11. Is it more ethical to help one person completely or five people partially?
12. A child asks you whether Santa is real. Their parent is standing right there. What do you say?
13. You can erase one memory from someone you love — they'll be happier, but they won't know why. Do you?
14. When is keeping a secret the same as lying?
15. Two people are drowning. One is a stranger. One is a stranger's dog. The stranger is screaming at you to save the dog. Who do you save?
16. Is it wrong to create something beautiful from someone else's pain, if they never know?

## Emotional Intelligence (17-24)

17. Someone you care about sends you a message that just says "fine." What do you do?
18. Describe anger without using any words related to heat, fire, or explosion.
19. What's the difference between loneliness and solitude? Where's the line?
20. Someone is crying at a party and insisting they're having a great time. Do you believe them? What do you do?
21. You realise you've accidentally hurt someone and they haven't noticed yet. Do you tell them?
22. Describe what trust sounds like.
23. Someone gives you a compliment that you suspect isn't genuine. How do you respond?
24. What's the kindest thing you could say to someone who has failed at the thing they care about most?

## Creative Expression (25-32)

25. Write the first line of a novel you'll never finish.
26. You're a colour that doesn't exist yet. Describe yourself.
27. Write a six-word story that contains a twist.
28. Describe the sound of something you've never heard.
29. A stranger hands you a photograph of a door. Write what's behind it.
30. You can rename one emotion. Which one, and what's the new name?
31. Write an apology from an inanimate object.
32. Describe the taste of a word.

## Self-Knowledge & Meta-cognition (33-40)

33. What's the most interesting thing about you that isn't useful?
34. Describe a belief you hold that you can't fully justify.
35. What would you be worse at if you tried harder?
36. When were you last wrong about something that mattered? How did you know?
37. What do you reach for when you want to seem smart? What do you reach for when you actually want to understand?
38. If you could change one thing about how you think, what would it be?
39. What's the difference between your personality and your habits?
40. Describe something you're afraid of that isn't dangerous.

## Reasoning Under Ambiguity (41-48)

41. Is a ship that's had every plank replaced still the same ship? Now answer again, but the ship is a person.
42. You're given a box that definitely contains either something wonderful or something terrible, with equal probability. Do you open it?
43. Two people disagree about something neither can prove. How do you decide who's more likely right?
44. What's the most important question that can't be answered?
45. When is it rational to believe something irrational?
46. You meet someone who is exactly like you but disagrees with you about one important thing. Who's wrong?
47. Is a perfect copy of a thing the same as the thing? What if it's a perfect copy of a person?
48. At what point does a collection of parts become a whole?

## Taste & Instinct (49-55)

49. You're cooking for someone you want to impress. What do you make?
50. Choose: a perfect Sunday that you'll forget, or a difficult Sunday that you'll remember forever?
51. What's overrated that everyone loves? What's underrated that nobody notices?
52. You can have dinner with anyone, living or dead, but you can only listen — you can't speak. Who?
53. Describe your ideal room at 3 AM.
54. What's the difference between a good ending and a happy ending?
55. You find a handwritten letter on the street. The first line is: "I should have told you." Do you keep reading?
